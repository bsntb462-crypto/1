Who Is Ibrahim Saleh?

I am Ibrahim Saleh — a digital entrepreneur and Co-Founder at The Founder Hub.

I did not choose entrepreneurship because it was the safe path. I chose it because I could not imagine spending my life building someone else's dream when I had my own burning inside me.

I have built ventures from zero, failed fast, learned faster, and kept going. Every project I launched taught me something that no classroom ever could. Every team I led showed me what real leadership looks like under pressure. Every hard decision I made shaped the founder I am today.

I am not here to talk about entrepreneurship. I am here to live it — every single day, with full commitment and zero shortcuts.

What I Believe In

I have spent years asking myself one question that refuses to leave me alone: why are we not using technology to make life genuinely easier for people — in every area, at every level?

That question became my compass. And it led me to two directions I am deeply committed to:

Technology for People — B2C

I believe the most powerful digital products are the ones that disappear into daily life so naturally that people cannot imagine living without them. Not apps for the sake of apps — but real solutions for real problems that millions of people face every single day.

I think about the mother who needs to manage her family's budget without a finance degree. The young patient who cannot afford to wait three weeks for a doctor's appointment. The small business owner running an entire operation from a single phone. The talented young person sitting on a skill that could feed a family — if only the right platform existed to connect them to the right opportunity.

These are not niche problems. These are the daily realities of millions of people across our region. And I believe that every single one of them is a product waiting to be built by a founder brave enough to care.

I also believe deeply in the power of digital government transformation. When I look at what Estonia built — a fully digital government where citizens can vote, register a business, access healthcare, and file taxes entirely online — I do not just see convenience. I see the foundation of a thriving startup ecosystem. When I look at what the UAE achieved with Dubai's smart city infrastructure, or what Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 is building at scale, or how Singapore integrated over 600 government services into a single citizen portal — I see what is possible when leadership decides that technology is not a luxury but a right.

Digital government does not just save people time. It eliminates corruption, creates transparency, opens equal access to opportunity, and signals to every investor in the world that this ecosystem is ready to be taken seriously. It creates entirely new markets for startups to serve alongside public infrastructure. It turns a young entrepreneur's idea into a business in hours instead of months.

That is the environment I want to help build — and the environment I want every founder I work with to launch inside.

Technology for Business — B2B

I am convinced that the single biggest untapped opportunity in the Arab world right now is sitting inside existing businesses that have not yet discovered what technology and artificial intelligence can do for them.

I have seen it firsthand. Companies with loyal customers, experienced teams, and real market presence — held back not by lack of ambition but by outdated systems, decisions made on gut feeling instead of data, and operations that consume far more human energy than they should.

I want to be the partner that changes that.

I am talking about AI-powered customer service that handles thousands of conversations simultaneously and still feels completely human. Predictive analytics that tell a business what to stock, what to price, and what to expect — before the market moves. Automation that frees talented people from repetitive work so they can do what only humans can do. Smart hiring and retention tools that make building a great team a science instead of a gamble. Supply chain visibility that turns chaos into clarity in real time.

The businesses that move on this in the next few years will not just grow faster. They will become impossible to compete with. And I intend to be right at the center of making that happen for every business ready to take the leap.

My Journey

Chapter One — The First Bet

I launched a CGI and VFX advertising production studio with an international remote team spread across multiple countries. My goal was simple and audacious: to produce the first Arab model for world-class visual advertising. We delivered over 7 advertising campaigns and secured two official partnerships — with Tashwish Marketing in Jeddah and Qa'id Marketing in Riyadh.

This chapter gave me one of the most valuable lessons of my life: a great idea without a solid financial structure and the right market timing is just a beautiful dream. I learned it the hard way — and I am grateful I did.

Chapter Two — Building for People, Not Profit

I led GrowUp — the first mobile app I took from a blank page to a complete, market-ready product. A daily companion that helps people build healthy habits, manage their money consciously, and move steadily toward their personal goals.

What made this project different was its soul. It was never about revenue. It was about genuine change in people's daily lives. I owned every stage — from the initial vision through UX design, technical specifications, and final delivery. It taught me that the products I care most about are the ones born from a real desire to serve people, not a spreadsheet.

Chapter Three — The Founder Hub

I joined as a Co-Founder at The Founder Hub — and this chapter feels like everything I have done before was preparation for exactly this.

The Founder Hub is built on one belief: that the Arab world is overflowing with extraordinary founders who are being held back not by lack of talent or ambition, but by lack of the right ecosystem. We are here to build that ecosystem — connecting bold ideas with the right investors, the right talent, and the strategic support that turns vision into reality.

What drives me most about this chapter is not what we are building. It is who we are building it for.

Chapter Four — The Standard I Hold Myself To

I study markets obsessively. I follow global startup ecosystems, track emerging technologies, and analyse investment trends not as a casual interest — but as a daily discipline.

I believe that the Arab entrepreneur who combines deep local understanding with genuine global thinking is the most powerful force this region has ever produced. That is not just something I believe about others. It is the standard I hold myself to — every single day.

Are You the Founder I Am Looking For?

I will be direct with you: I am not easy to work with if you are looking for someone to just agree with your ideas and stay out of your way.

But if you are looking for a co-founder who will be as invested in your vision as you are — someone who brings strategic depth, real field experience, and the kind of honest partnership that actually builds things — then I might be exactly who you need.

I am looking for someone specific. Someone who wakes up thinking about their idea. Someone who cannot stop building even when everyone around them does not understand it yet. Someone who is genuinely willing to sacrifice comfort, certainty, and shortcuts for something they deeply believe in.

I am looking for someone with a vision, a story they are fighting to make real, and the discipline to show up for it every single day — not just on the good days.

Your idea does not need to be perfect. Your commitment does.

If that sounds like you — I am ready to have a real conversation.
